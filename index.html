Overview

How our solution can help

Have you ever notice an interesting art piece, but felt confused or lost?
You try to understand its meaning and purpose,
but you are confounded and have no idea how to start.

With the interactive experience that we’ve designed, we introduce
a novel way to guide you through an art piece and spark a moment
of joy. This in turn positively contributes to the livability of a city.









Problem




How might we spark a moment of joy or reflection when visiting public art?










Methods

   Observe and Intercept

     Contextual Inquiry

      Think-Aloud

    Storyboards

   Speed-Dating

   Experience Prototyping







Insights and Evidence


Preference for Guidance

Visitors prefer trusted guidance on how to interpret an art piece, as well as the possibility of having personal gain after interaction.

“Abstract pieces are more confusing ... I am comfortable with some form of hand holding to help me appreciate the art”



Impact of Evident Cues

Visitors mentioned that they needed visual cues to notice an art piece. This could be in multiple forms, such as new
art pieces, novelty in an existing art piece, or even incomplete art pieces that engage viewers to complete them.

“We work nearby and never saw this before, we were curious so came to check it out”



Low Effort and Time for Engagement

Visitors are willing to interact with an art piece when it requires minimal effort and time.

“I will interact with it only for 30 seconds to 1 minute, otherwise I will move on”




Solution

To best design this experience, we consulted art curators and installers
to learn the techniques and guidelines they follow to create engaging
installations. We also observed and interviewed art viewers in order to
understand their struggles and desires when visiting an art piece.

Synthesizing our findings, we designed an experience where we projected
different animated images and facts about the art piece on the ground.
This experience invited visitors to step on the screens in order to
interact with the projection.

This solution addresses the need for guidance in a simple yet exciting
way to help visitors interpret public art and create a novel experience.
